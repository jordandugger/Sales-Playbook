The Framework
How this objection works
Before you run any step
When overcoming money/financial objections, you must first identify whether the prospect is giving you an emotional money objection (fear, uncertainty, lack of belief in themselves) or a logistical money objection (they genuinely don't have access to the funds right now) — or a combination of both.

Isolating the objection lets you enter the conversation with confidence, knowing exactly what you're actually handling.
The goal: Handle emotional, fear, and uncertainty objections BEFORE you ever offer a payment plan option. Handling finances last — only once conviction is locked — allows logistical money objections to resolve themselves easily.
Triple Tie-Down
The sequence — step by step
Tie Down #1
Isolate the True Objection
SAY THIS

"Investment aside, is there anything else that would keep you less than 100% certain that you would want to work with us to help you get to [XYZ GOAL]?"

"Or is it just a cash flow thing?"

If they name another concern — address it directly before continuing. The goal is to confirm: the only thing left is the money. Once you have that, move to Tie Down #2.
Tie Down #2
If you had it, would you do it?
SAY THIS

"I'm curious — if you had $[PRICE] completely liquid to you right now... would you do it?"

"Would you say you're 10 out of 10 on the process specifically?"

You need a yes here before moving forward. If they hesitate on the process itself — you have a different objection. Surface it now. If they say yes, you've confirmed it's purely logistical. Now you have permission to help them find a way.
Permission
Bridge to Open Wallet
SAY THIS

"Got it — the last thing we want is for finances to be the thing that holds you back from getting the help you need."

"I do have some flexibility here to create something custom for you to make it more digestible. I don't have an option A or option B or anything set in stone..."

"...so, do you mind if I ask you a personal question?"

Wait for them to say yes before asking anything. This micro-permission keeps the conversation collaborative — they invited you in, you're not prying.
Open Wallet
Cash on Hand
SAY THIS

"Again, just so I can see if I can help — what would you have available to you today in terms of cash on hand in your checking, savings, or even credit via credit cards?"

"Just so I can try to create something more digestible for you."

Bucket 1
Checking
Bucket 2
Savings
Bucket 3
Credit
Probe each bucket specifically. Don't stop at the first number. "And in savings?" / "Any room on credit cards?" — you're building a complete picture, not accepting the first answer as final.
Tie Down #3
Close on Custom Plan
SAY THIS

"I appreciate you being open and honest with me — let me ask you this."

"If I was able to break this up and allow you in today — we'll still treat you like you paid in full."

"If I was able to make this more digestible for you today and create something custom based on what you have, would you be ready to get started today?"

This is the close. You're not asking if they want to do it — you already have that from Tie Down #2. You're only asking if a custom structure makes it possible. Make it easy to say yes.
⚠ Critical Rules — Never Break These
Never offer a payment plan before running Tie Down #1 and #2. Offering a plan before you've confirmed conviction rewards hesitation and creates a price negotiation, not a commitment.
If they can't say they're 10/10 on the process in Tie Down #2, stop. You have a different objection hiding under the money objection. Find it first.
Never book a follow-up to "figure out finances." A follow-up doesn't fix a financial objection — it just gives it more time to solidify. Close it on the call or lose the deal.
