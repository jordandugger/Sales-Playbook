The Foundation
The Enrollment Advisor Creed
Our only job as an enrollment advisor
"Our job is not to sell.
Our job is to serve."
01
To help our prospects make the best decision for themselves — or to fully understand whether we can actually help this person achieve their desired outcome.
02
If we can help them — it's holding them accountable to their own desire and ambition. Not letting them off the hook when the discomfort of change shows up.
The fundamental problem: People will fight like hell to stay in their comfort zone. The best enrollment advisors don't manipulate prospects into buying — they help them make the uncomfortable decision they already know they need to make. Diagnose first. Prescribe second. Never the other way around.
The Scripts
Choose the right syntax for the call
Script 01
Starting from scratch · < $2K/month
Goal Syntax Script
For prospects with no established coaching business or under $2K/month in consistent revenue. The problem is the absence of a business. Discovery builds desire and surfaces the cost of inaction. Pitch from aspiration — not problem-solving.
Use this when
Prospect is in a job, just starting out, or has under $2K/month with no traction. Fear and uncertainty are the dominant emotions.
Open Script
Script 02
Established coaches · $2K–$40K+/month
Problem Syntax Script
For coaches with an existing business and a specific, identifiable constraint — traffic, pipeline, close rate, or retention. Diagnose the leak first. Every pillar problem-links directly back to their exact constraint.
Use this when
Prospect has real revenue but something specific is broken or capped. They know the problem — your job is to quantify it and position IFCA as the precise fix.
Open Script
Script 03
In-person trainers → hybrid / online
PT Pivot Script
For in-person trainers transitioning to hybrid or fully online. The Three Currencies framework is the belief-breaker. Lead with the physical and time ceiling — not just the income gap.
Use this when
Prospect is a credentialed in-person trainer wanting to go online. Address "no audience, no ads budget, no idea how to deliver" before they become objections.
